The Arts Council National Lottery Project Grants 10/07/2020, updated 27/07/2020, 5/10/2020, 29/3/2021 £77.9 million The Arts Council has announced that its National Lottery Project Grants programme will reopen on 22 July. The fund will support individuals, artists, organisations, museums and libraries. We have prioritised reopening this programme and designed it to respond flexibly to the needs of independent organisations, creative practitioners and freelancers. England In response to the Covid-19 crisis, the programme will be prioritising the needs of individual artists and creative practitioners, and cultural organisations which contribute to the resilience of the national sector, venues, museums, and libraries, and grassroots music activity and touring, for grants under £100,000. Grants of between £1,500 and £100,000 will be awarded as part of a £59.9 million rolling funding programme until April 2021 to support those in the cultural sector and for work that has a demonstrably positive impact such as Research and Development. High number of applications are expected therefore applications offer a timed permission process.
Applicants will be notified of the outcome of their application within six weeks (for applications under £15,000) and 12 weeks (for applications over £15,000). Please note that the number of applications we receive may not always be manageable and we will always prioritise those that demonstrate a strong need for support.

The Foyle Foundation Main Grants Scheme 23/07/2020 Not known Launched by the Government’s support package for the Arts sector, The Foyle Foundation has re-opened its Main Grants Scheme to support charities in the UK that have been operating for a minimum of three years and to consider applications related to the Arts or Learning, and also taking into consideration of the needs of the different circumstances since March 2020 lockdown. UK Wide Arts or Learning Charities with an income of more than £150,000 per annum who can demonstrate that they have been operating for a minimum of 3 years. The majority of Main Grants are expected to be in the range of £10,000 to £50,000 for revenue or capital purposes. The Trust will consider larger grants (and multi-year grants) depending upon need and purpose, favouring those with significant core costs, existing public funding and no other applicable source. Online applications are accepted all year round, there are no deadlines for submission.
The Foyle Foundation Small Grants Scheme 08/06/2020 updated 5/10/2020 Not Known The Foyle Foundation re-opened its Small Grants Scheme to support smaller, grass roots and local charities in the UK. UK Wide Charities with a turnover of less than £150,000 per annum and who can demonstrate the Stability and clear purpose of their services, are able to deliver services directly to the community, and intent on supporting vulnerable and disadvantaged people in the community, either directly or through online support. Grants of between £1,000 and £10,000 can be used to cover core costs or essential equipment, to enable ongoing service delivery or to support delivery of online or digital services. County Durham Community Foundation COVID 19 Fund - Closing soon 30/03/2020 updated 5/10/2020, 12/04/2021 Not known The COVID 19 Fund has been set up to support community groups and organisations through this period of uncertainty. Our focus is to offer financial support to community and voluntary groups as a result of the continuing threat of coronavirus. This will be to directly support a group’s work in their community to ensure that people and organisations survive over the next period, and to help organisations diversify their community activities. County Durham, Darlington, Tees Valley Groups must:
• Be based in County Durham, Darlington or Tees Valley
• Be a voluntary organisation with at least 3 unrelated signatories (two unrelated signatories)
• Have a bank account in the organisation’s name (with two named contacts)
• Have a charity or other profit account for small, informal/unrelated groups		 Up to £5,000 is available to support the following activities:
• Organisations to support older people and other vulnerable groups affected by the COVID 19 outbreak
• Additional costs of working remotely and adapting services delivered in support of community coordinated responses to the COVID 19 outbreak
• Activities to support community coordinated responses to the COVID 19 outbreak
• Initiatives working to combat poverty (e.g. initiatives to tackle food poverty or those closing the gap in existing statutory support such as providing food and essential goods, signposting and putting authorities in place and giving emotional support).		 The Fund opened to applications on Monday 30th March 2020 and applications to this fund will be assessed on an ongoing basis subject to available funds. You can access the online application form here.
NB the Covid-19 Fund, set up to support community groups and organisations through the initial period of lockdown, will be closing soon. Support for community groups and organisations, both short term and long term, remains available from our Community Grants Programme. The next deadline for applications to this fund is Monday 5 July 2021. - Closed

England & Wales older people (who are of state pension age or older living on the equivalent) living on less than £4,500 per year after essential bills for other funders.
